Circulation Services

Circulation Service - Library Circulation is the function of lending library materials (books, serials, sound recordings, moving images, cartographic materials, etc. that are owned by a library) to the users of the library. Library Circulation includes checking out library materials to library users, renewing the borrowed items, reserving checked out items for the patron, checking in materials returned, checking the materials for damage at the time of return, if found damaged then giving that to responsible staff for repair and when repair is not possible then replacement, renewal of materials, receiving payment of fines for damaged and overdue materials and payment for subscription to the library and other charges, maintaining order in the stacks by re-shelving the library materials by call number given by classification system, such as Library of Congress Classification (LCC) or Dewey Decimal Classification (DDC) system. There is a Circulation Desk, a long counter usually located at the main entrance of the library to carry out library circulation activities. It is the service point to register for the library card, check out, renew, and return library materials.Borrow books easily with our efficient system. Enjoy access to a wide variety of genres and authors.

Archives and Record Service

A record is a record of information that is created or maintained by an organization or institution that is necessary for the day-to-day activities of an organization or institution to carry out the purpose for which it was established. Records are records or documents that are passed down from generation to generation as historical evidence and transferred to churches for permanent preservation as an early source of information for research and development. There is no question that properly managed and managed records are the main instrument for good governance and the development of democratic order, the success of institutions, the development and growth of the nation, the spread of education and knowledge, the flourishing and development of national sentiment. If records are properly collected, organized and preserved, they will play an irreplaceable role in the overall development of the country. Churches, churches and documentation centers are at the forefront of obtaining registered and protected records. The National Archives and Library Agency of Ethiopia is a governmental institution established under Proclamation 179/99 that is fulfilling its responsibilities to collect, preserve and preserve historical records and pass them on to future generations and serve as a resource for research and development. Digital Library Service

Digital Library Services provides a wide array of services to assist members of the library with organizing collections of materials or making them more widely available. the following services offered by the Digital Libraries.

  • Catalogue Databases,
  • Current Awareness Bulletins,
  • Internally Published Newsletters, Reports & Journals,
  • Electronic Table of Contents,
  • Electronic Document Delivery Service,
  • Electronic Theses and Dissertations,
  • Electronic Publishing,
  • Central storage facilities for Hosting digital collections and indexes
  • Tools for loading, storing, searching, and displaying digital objects
  • Special Collections service

ABOUT US

The Ethiopian National Archives and Library Agency is a governmental body established by proclamation No.179/1999 as a legal frame work and a nation-wide level of responsibilities. It was set up in 1943 with the name public library- wemezekir. The Agency has been delivering lots of services such as research and study services, reading services, training services, micro film reading services audio, audio-video recording services and consultancy services particularly to the researchers, scholars, academicians, writers and the public at large to create information informed society.
